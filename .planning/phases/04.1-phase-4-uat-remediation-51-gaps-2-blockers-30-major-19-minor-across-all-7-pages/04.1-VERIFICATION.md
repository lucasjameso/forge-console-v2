### Gaps Summary

Two functional gaps prevent full goal achievement -- both are forms that have complete UI but whose submit handlers are missing mutation wiring:

**Gap 1: Add Content form does not persist (UAT-CONTENT-ADD-MODAL)**
The AddContentDialog in ContentPipeline.tsx was redesigned with a full 8-field form (Post Body, CharCountIndicator, ContentType, platform multi-select, auto week number, series, notes). However `handleCreate` (line 186) only fires a toast and resets the form -- no mutation is called. The content item is silently discarded. This is a functional regression: the old basic form also didn't persist, but the UAT requirement asks for a working Add Content flow.

**Gap 2: Log Activity form does not persist (UAT-ACTIVITY-MANUAL-ENTRY)**
The manual entry form in ActivityLog.tsx was enhanced with tool dropdown, event type dropdown, and link URL input. However `handleLogSubmit` (line 231) resets state with an explicit TODO comment: "In a real implementation, this would call a mutation". The form UI satisfies the visual requirement but the action has no effect on the activity log data.

Both gaps share the same root cause: the plans specified UI design but the persistence mutations were either not available or not wired. The fixes require either a `useCreateContent` mutation hook (for ContentPipeline) and a `useCreateActivityEntry` mutation hook (for ActivityLog), or alternatively wiring to existing mutation infrastructure.
